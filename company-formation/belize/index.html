Belize

IBC Formation

The Belize International Business Company (IBC) is formed under the recent 1990 Belize IBC Act and is considered a modern and user-friendly offshore jurisdiction. A Belize IBC is an ideal corporate vehicle for international financial trade as the regulations and processes have been designed with the offshore investor in mind. Belize's laws facilitate quick and efficient incorporation and is a good jurisdiction for small, start-up international businesses.


History

Belize, formerly a British Colony, is the only country in Central America with English as its official language. Nestled in the Yucatan peninsula, Belize is merely 200 miles south of beautiful Cancun in Mexico, and less than 100 miles east of the famous Tikal Ruins of Guatemala.


The Benefits

  • Nil Taxes payable – profits and income arising from the company are not subject to tax so long as the trade was not within Belize.
  • There is no requirement for a secretary resident or otherwise.
  • Only one director or shareholder required for the company formation.
  • The shareholder(s) and director(s) can be a natural person or a corporate body.
  • The Belize International Business Company does not pay any tax on its worldwide profits to the Belize Authorities. Therefore no taxes or stamp duties are applicable to this structure.
